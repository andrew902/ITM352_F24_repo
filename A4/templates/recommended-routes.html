Popular Routes in Honolulu

  • Waikiki Beach -Begin at Kapiʻolani Regional Park and head north via the waterfront path or roadside sidewalk. Follow Kalākaua Avenue and the Sheraton/Halekulani Boardwalk, enjoying coastal views, surfers, and bustling shops. After 1.4 miles, arrive at Fort DeRussy Park, where you can extend your run with a ~1-mile inland loop featuring an outdoor gym. Continue on the Fort DeRussy Boardwalk to complete a 1/2-mile loop around the lagoon. For the return, retrace your route or run along Kalākaua Avenue to explore Waikiki's vibrant atmosphere.
  • Diamond Head Loop - This scenic loop circles Honolulu’s iconic Diamond Head Volcano, located just south of Waikiki Beach. You can choose between a 4.6-mile perimeter loop or a challenging 1.6-mile roundtrip run to the summit, which includes a 560-foot elevation gain. Along the way, enjoy views of upscale homes, city parks, surfers on the beaches below, and even humpback whales during migration season.
  • Ala Moana Park and Magic Island -Ala Moana Park features a beach and grassy areas popular with Oahu athletes, especially on evenings and weekends. Located 3 miles east of downtown and 2 miles west of Waikiki Beach, the park offers a network of sidewalks for running along the beach, through the main park, and around Magic Island. The flat, paved loop is about 3 miles long. Free parking is available within the park and near the marina, though spots can be limited due to high demand from surfers.
  • Ala Wai Canal - Enjoy a scenic waterside run just north of Waikiki Beach along the Ala Wai Canal and park, which parallels the river on Ala Wai Boulevard. This well-maintained path is popular among local runners due to light traffic and great views of paddlers in outrigger canoes. There’s also a running track nearby. For a longer route, consider linking this path with the Ala Moana Regional Park or Waikiki Beach routes.Begin at Ala Wai Park near the elementary school and run toward Date Street. Turn right and follow the path along the golf course until you reach Kapahulu Avenue. Make another right and continue along Ala Wai Boulevard, enjoying the lush greenery and water views. The path transitions from an asphalt bike trail to a cement sidewalk along Kapahulu Avenue.
  • Kapiolani Regional Park - This 2-mile loop is a great extension or alternative to the Diamond Head or Waikiki Beach runs. Key highlights include the palm-lined Kalākaua Avenue and the banyan-shaded Paki Avenue. Within Kapiʻolani Regional Park, you can run along Kalākaua Avenue or follow the waterfront path near the Aquarium for scenic ocean views.
